## Top 5 Coaching Recommendations

1. **Open with a named buyer consequence, not a product category.** The first 60 seconds must establish a specific, painful business situation. No product UI until the viewer has a reason to care.

2. **Complete the ODI arc in every demo: ingest, transform, consume.** Show Fivetran moving data, dbt running a model that surfaces a business metric, and that metric appearing in a dashboard or AI query. The connector configuration is setup, not the story.

3. **End with a specific, concrete next step and say it out loud.** A demo without a CTA is a presentation. "Here is the documentation link — I'll send it after the call" is a next step. "Thank you for watching" is not.

4. **Rehearse to a clean resolution before recording.** If the demo requires Docker, pre-pull the image. If it requires an agent, pre-register it. A broken demo with accurate commentary about product reliability produces cognitive dissonance that damages the deal.

5. **Name the competitive alternative and explain specifically why Fivetran wins.** In a Databricks context, address Lakeflow Connect directly. In a regulated enterprise, address HVR. Generic "no code, no API" does not answer "but we already have X."

## What a Perfect 100-Point Demo Looks Like

Open with a named persona and a specific, quantified consequence — a regulation, a cost, a missed deadline. No product UI for the first 60 seconds.

Show a complete three-layer pipeline: Fivetran moves data (Proxy Agent or Hybrid, egress-only explained in two sentences), dbt transforms it (model runs live or in playback, bronze-to-gold, business metric emerges), a consumption layer (dashboard, AI agent, dbt Wizard query) answers a business question using that data.

Weave in objection handling. Justin's five-way connection method comparison is the right structure: name the alternatives, state the honest trade-off for each, recommend one. Chris's revocation line ("stop the agent, no firewall changes") is a one-liner that should be in every security-focused demo. Name the competitive alternative the buyer is already evaluating.

Calibrate depth to the buyer in the room. DBA gets BLR mechanics. CDO gets the open-format portability story. Same demo, different emphasis layer.

Close by summarizing what was proved, not what was shown. State a specific next step out loud. The total runtime is under 20 minutes and the demo works on the first attempt.
